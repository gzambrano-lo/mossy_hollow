Why We Love Cute Things

Mossy Hollow began with a simple question: why do tiny things make us so happy?

Researchers who study cuteness, often called Cute Studies or Kawaii Studies, have found that people are naturally drawn to soft shapes, small sizes, round features, and gentle expressions. These traits trigger feelings of comfort, care, and affection. Psychologist Konrad Lorenz referred to many of these features as the "baby schema," a collection of traits that encourage nurturing and protective responses.

The Science of Cozy

Studies suggest that cute imagery does more than make us smile. Looking at cute objects can encourage attentiveness, carefulness, and feelings of social connection. Researchers have also found that kawaii, the Japanese concept often translated as "cute," extends beyond appearance alone. It can include friendliness, harmlessness, softness, warmth, and a sense of emotional comfort.
